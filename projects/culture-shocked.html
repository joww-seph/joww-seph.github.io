Culture Shocked

A 2D educational adventure game celebrating Philippine indigenous and traditional cultural heritage. Explore a virtual museum, learn from Lola NPCs, and master traditional crafts through interactive mini-games. In Development.

Overview

Culture Shocked is a 2D top-down educational adventure game that celebrates Philippine indigenous and traditional cultural heritage. Players explore a virtual museum filled with 50+ interactive artifacts from diverse Filipino ethnic groups, receive guidance from Lola (grandmother) NPC characters, and participate in hands-on traditional craft mini-games. The game transforms cultural education into an engaging, interactive experience that bridges cultural preservation with digital engagement.

Museum Exploration

The game features 50+ interactive cultural artifacts spanning multiple Philippine indigenous traditions, each accompanied by educational information panels and NPC guidance:

  • Textiles: T’nalak (T’boli), Inabel (Ilocano), Tapis (Visayan), Inaul (Maguindanao), Hablon (Hiligaynon), Yakan (Basilan)
  • Weapons & Shields: Barong, Kampilan, Kalasag, Kris, Panabas
  • Musical Instruments: Kutyapi, Kulintang, Gangsa, Kubing
  • Decorative & Ritual Items: Bulul statues, Okir carvings, Burnay pottery, Salakot

Interactive Mini-Games

Three fully immersive craft simulations rooted in authentic Filipino traditions:

  • Inabel Weaving: Simulates the traditional Ilocano textile weaving process with authentic steps, from thread selection, treadle operation, heddle manipulation, shuttle control, and step-by-step guided progression.
  • Bilao (Basket) Weaving: A bamboo basket-weaving simulation with drag-and-drop mechanics for weaving patterns, bamboo stripping as a precursor step, and circular basket completion sequence.
  • Batok (Tattoo Art): Simulates the Kalinga hand-tapping tattoo tradition with brush mechanics, ink-dipping interaction, tapping simulation, and pattern-based drawing canvas for creating traditional designs.

Design philosophy: Gameplay mechanics mirror real craft processes, giving players actual procedural knowledge of each craft, not just abstract simulation.

Design Philosophy

  • Edutainment approach: Gameplay mechanics mirror real craft processes, giving players actual procedural knowledge, not just abstract simulation.
  • NPC as cultural bridge: Lola (grandmother) characters serve as guides, grounding the experience in Filipino intergenerational storytelling traditions.
  • Pixel-art aesthetic: Integer-scaled 640×360 rendering targets a clean, retro-inspired visual style consistent with indie educational games.
  • Accessibility: GL Compatibility renderer ensures the game runs on a wide range of hardware, including lower-end devices.
  • Cultural authenticity: Artifacts and craft mechanics are researched and designed with respect for their cultural origins.
