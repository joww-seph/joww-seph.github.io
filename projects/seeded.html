SeedEd

An AI-Powered Learning Pod for Nurturing Curiosities

A portable, inflatable dome classroom equipped with offline AI, 360-degree projection, and solar-powered electricity — deployable to remote and rural communities with no access to quality education.

Project PDF
Add a screenshot here

Overview

SeedEd is an AI-powered learning pod that aims to improve literacy and provide foundational knowledge to children living in remote areas where quality education is inaccessible. The system takes the form of a portable, inflatable dome — 3 meters high and 6 meters in diameter — that creates a planetarium-like immersive classroom experience.

Presented under the MindSpheres team from Mariano Marcos State University, SeedEd won the GCD4FE Southeast Asia Regionals. The concept combines a fine-tuned offline LLaMa AI model, 360-degree dome projection, solar-powered energy, and a mobile progress tracking app to deliver self-contained education without relying on internet connectivity or permanent infrastructure.

The Problem

Global literacy remains a critical and unresolved crisis. According to UNESCO:

Statistic Figure
Adults globally who remain illiterate 754 million
Children not enrolled in school 251 million
Children in lower-income countries unable to read by age 10 57%

The core barriers are inaccessibility to education in remote areas, high illiteracy rates, poor learning resources, and the absence of engaging, adaptive classroom environments.

The Solution

SeedEd addresses these barriers through a fully self-contained portable learning system with four core components:

  • Inflatable dome classroom — a portable, 3M × 6M dome with 360-degree projection creates an immersive planetarium-like experience deployable anywhere
  • Offline AI learning assistant — a fine-tuned LLaMa AI model that personalizes lessons, tracks progress, and adapts to each student's learning pace — all without internet
  • Solar-powered electricity — fully solar-powered battery system eliminates dependency on grid infrastructure
  • Systematic evaluation system — structured 10-level literacy progression system based on Bloom's Taxonomy and AVK learning methods

Technology Stack

  • LLaMa AI (fine-tuned) — core AI model for adaptive lesson generation, student progress tracking, and content personalization
  • SQLite — lightweight local database for offline student information and progress storage
  • Speech-to-text — maximizes auditory interaction for verbal learners
  • Text-to-speech — supports auditory learning delivery
  • Interactive tablet — hands-on interface for kinesthetic learners
  • 360-degree projection system — dome projection for immersive visual content
  • Solar panel system — sustainable off-grid power source
  • Mobile app — progress tracking and session monitoring for educators

Deployment Workflow

  1. Deploy — transport and inflate the SEED dome in a target remote or rural area
  2. Set up — configure the system for 1–2 weeks of sessions
  3. Teach — use the fine-tuned offline LLaMa AI model to personalize lessons and track student progress
  4. Generate — create new AI-generated content for follow-up learning sessions
  5. Update — fine-tune and retrain the LLaMa model based on session data when back online
  6. Return — deflate SEED and transport to the next deployment location

10-Level Literacy Progression

The SeedEd curriculum is structured into 10 progressive literacy levels grounded in Bloom's Taxonomy:

Level Skill Taxonomy
1 Recognize Remember
2 Recall Words Remember
3 Comprehend Understand
4 Apply Words in Context Apply
5 Short Stories Retelling Understand / Apply
6 Basic Analysis Analyze
7 Evaluate Evaluate
8 Creative Expression Create
9 Synthesize Knowledge Analyze / Evaluate / Create
10 Independent Communication Create

Feasibility & Impact

  • Educational impact — Very High: a fully immersive experience that directly combats illiteracy at the foundational level
  • Scalability — Medium: one dome serves multiple barangays sequentially through a rotation deployment model
  • Community engagement — Strong: designed for partnerships with local governments, DepEd, NGOs, and international academic institutions
  • Sustainability — High: equipment and AI models are reused and fine-tuned across deployments, minimizing recurring costs

What I Learned

As Team Captain and Head Project Manager, this project pushed me far beyond software development. I coordinated a multidisciplinary team — spanning feasibility analysis, financial modeling, foreign affairs, and design — and had to align all of it around a coherent product vision. The biggest challenge was bridging the gap between ambitious technology ideas and real-world deployment constraints in resource-limited communities.

Designing for offline-first AI taught me how much assumptions about connectivity shape typical systems. We had to rethink every layer: storage (SQLite over cloud), learning models (fine-tuned LLaMa instead of API-dependent models), and power (solar instead of grid). This project also deepened my understanding of education research — embedding Bloom's Taxonomy and AVK methods into an AI curriculum required translating pedagogy into structured data, which was entirely new territory for me.
